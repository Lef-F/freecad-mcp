## Daylight (Dagsljus / Ljusförhållanden) — BFS 2024:8, kap. 4

**Legal basis**: PBL 8 kap. 4 § + PBF 3 kap. 9 § → BFS 2024:8 (Boverkets föreskrifter om skydd med hänsyn till hygien, hälsa och miljö)

### Dwelling daylight requirements

| BFS 2024:8 ref | Rule | Value | Type | Applies to |
|----------------|------|-------|------|------------|
| 4 kap. 1 § | Dagsljusfaktor (DF) minst 1,0 % för halva den sammanlagda bedömda ytan av alla vistelserum i bostaden | Daylight factor ≥1.0% for at least half the total assessed area of all habitable rooms in the dwelling | **DF ≥ 1.0%** | Krav | Bostäder |
| 4 kap. 2 § | Utblick krävs i rum för samvaro och måltider i bostäder | View out required in living/dining rooms | — | Krav | Bostäder |
| 4 kap. 3 § | Lokaler: krav på tillräckligt dagsljus och utblick i vistelserum | Workplaces/premises: sufficient daylight and view required in habitable rooms | — | Krav | Lokaler |
| 4 kap. 4 § | Fast installerad belysning ska vara anpassad till avsedd användning och hälsosam | Fixed installed lighting must be adapted to intended use and healthy | — | Krav | All buildings |
| 13 kap. 5 § | Vid ändring av byggnader: dagsljusfaktor ≥0,8 % kan godtas | Renovation: DF ≥0.8% acceptable (not lower) | **DF ≥ 0.8%** | Krav (adapted) | Existing buildings |

### Daylight factor (dagsljusfaktor) explained

- **Definition**: Ratio of indoor illuminance to simultaneous outdoor illuminance under overcast sky (CIE overcast sky)
- **1% DF** = 1/100th of outdoor daylight enters the room
- Measured/calculated over assessed floor area of each habitable room
- "Halva den bedömda ytan" = at least half of each room's assessed area must achieve DF ≥1.0%
- **New 2025 vs old BBR**: Old rule required DF at one point (roughly centre) in each room. New rule: aggregate across all rooms — one room can be darker if others compensate
- No requirement for direct daylight — indirect (reflected, fibre optic) is acceptable if DF ≥1.0%

### What counts as "vistelserum" (habitable room)
- Living rooms (vardagsrum), dining rooms (matrum), bedrooms (sovrum), kitchens (kök)
- NOT: bathrooms, toilets, storage, hallways (only temporary stay)

### Shared housing (gemensamhetsboende)
Per BFS 2024:8, 1 kap. 5 §: DF ≥1.0% required BOTH for individual unit (boendeenhet) AND for shared common spaces (delade funktioner).

### Lokaler (non-residential premises)
- Qualitative requirement: "tillräckligt dagsljus" (sufficient daylight) and "utblick" (view out)
- No specific DF% specified — professional assessment required
- Exceptions: hotel rooms (temporary sleep only), large industrial halls, shops in underground stations, archives, exhibitions where daylight is unsuitable

### Fixed lighting (fast installerad belysning) — BFS 2024:8, 4 kap. 4 §
If fixed lighting exists, it must:
- Be adapted to the intended use
- Not cause unacceptable health risk from: light intensity, colour rendering (färgåtergivning), flicker (flimmer), glare (bländning), light distribution, contrast

### Renovation / change of use
- Converting non-residential to residential: DF ≥0.8% minimum (lower not accepted)
- Exception for buildings with large cultural-historical value (e.g. water towers, silos) — further adaptation possible per 13 kap. 5 § stycke 2
- Does NOT apply to converting a former dwelling back to dwelling use

## Windows (Fönster)

> BBR does not specify window dimensions directly — requirements are derived from:
> - Daylight: DF ≥1.0% (see above)
> - Ventilation: openable area per BBR avsnitt 6 (hygien)
> - Evacuation: window opening ≥0.5 m² (height × width) at ≤23 m for window egress
> - Accessibility: view from seated/standing position
